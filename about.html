About Me

My Journey

I'm Harshita Sharma, an interior design student at Shri Vaishnav Institute of Architecture with a passion for creating thoughtful, human-centered spaces. My academic journey has been shaped by a deep interest in how design can improve lives, especially for vulnerable populations like children.

With a strong foundation in design principles, spatial planning, and sustainable practices, I approach every project with curiosity and rigor. Whether it's through detailed CAD documentation, material research, or conceptual visualization, I believe in merging creativity with problem-solving.

My Design Philosophy

  • Human-Centered Design: Every space I design starts with the people who will use it. Understanding user needs, behaviors, and aspirations is central to my approach.
  • Sustainability and Responsibility: I'm committed to eco-friendly materials and practices that minimize environmental impact without compromising aesthetics or function.
  • Research-Driven: I don't just design; I investigate. Deep research into context, culture, and precedent informs every decision I make.
  • Clarity and Communication: Great design is communicated clearly. I use drawings, renderings, and documentation to ensure my vision is understood by clients and stakeholders.

Notable Work

Kid's Library — Thesis Project

My thesis project explores the design of inclusive, child-friendly learning spaces. Through extensive research into child development, pedagogical principles, and environmental psychology, I developed a comprehensive masterplan for a library that prioritizes:

  • Age-appropriate spatial zoning and furniture proportions
  • Natural light and biophilic design elements for cognitive development
  • Accessible, safe, and engaging materiality
  • Interactive and flexible spaces for collaborative learning
  • Sustainable, non-toxic materials and practices

This project represents my commitment to designing spaces that serve real human needs with thoughtfulness and rigor.
